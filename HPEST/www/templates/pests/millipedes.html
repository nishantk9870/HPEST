Millipedes

Millipedes are slow-crawling arthropods belonging to the class Diplopoda. Approximately 10,000 species exist worldwide. Also called “thousand-leggers,” millipedes earn recognition for their long, rounded bodies and scores of legs. Despite having a name that means “1,000-footed” in Latin, millipedes actually have fewer than 100 legs in most cases. Though not harmful, millipedes can secrete a foul-smelling toxin if agitated.
